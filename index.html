SingVEX
#Robotics
#STEM
#Engineering
#Leadership
The SingVEX robotics competition was an annual competition in that teaches students about advanced robotics concepts and encourages innovation. The robot is made of metal which can be cut into size to fit the robot.
The 2023 SIngVEX season was SpinUp, where robots have to shoot discs into high goals or low goals to score points. The game format was a 2v2 where robots compete for as many scores as possible.
Without any prior experience to VEX, I believe our team gave our all in this competition and achieved top 16th nationally. We stayed came back to the robotics room whenever we could to improve, tinker and fix our robots. We created a robot that could rival the power house teams in a short span of 3 months.
This was definitely an experience of a lifetime for me. Because of the short deadline, we understood that getting a working robot out in time was imperative, we innovated rapidly, speeding through 3 different robot iterations with no time to waste.
Being the main coder, builder and driver of the robot, this competition taught me the importance of balance, innovation, and decision making
To learn more, please watch the short robot montage

SST-SUTD Camp
#STEM
#Engineering
#Leadership
The SUTD Big-D camp was a camp held in collaboration with SUTD and SST, to encourage and nurture an engineering mindset. It was a short 3 day camp where students are required to ideate and create a prototype that solve a real world problem - urban disasters.
My group and I decided to work on the problem of water scarcity. In the short span of 3 days, we learnt industry relevant skills, such as 3d modelling, 3d printing, soldering and laser cutting.
After 3 days of headache and stress, our group was able to come up with a working viable solution to our problem, and presented it to the judges. Allowing us to clinch the Design Excellence Award which was the most prestigious award, given to the team that has shown excellence and balance in all fields of design.
I believe that our design has gone above and beyond, showing good consideration in more than areas of user experience, urban planning, innovation and sustainability.
As the team leader and 3d modeller, I learnt the importance of adaptability and leadership. Especially when your solution is not working, i had to think of new ways to salvage the design and instruct my teammates to take the necessary steps with respect to time to fix it.

2022 TechForGood Festival
#STEM
#Engineering
#Leadership
#VIA
The Tech for good competition was an annual competition aimed to help the less fortunate. We chose the target audience of intellectually disabled and made an app to help them train social awareness.
In collaboration with MINDS and T4G, we created Friendly. Friendly was an AI-based Intelligent Social Companion app is developed based on Kivy, a Cross-Platform Python Framework. The app aimed to cultivate better social skills and to improve the social behavior of persons with Intellectual Disability (ID). The developed solution has 3 major features:
  - [Conversation Simulator] The conversation simulator aims to cultivate socially acceptable behaviors within PWIDS through remote means. Provides a conversation prompt and grades the response according to how appropriate it is.
  - [Sign Language Neural Network] A sign language recognition system is developed using Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) Deep Learning model and optimized with oneAPI Deep Neural Network Library (oneDNN).
  - [Anti Spam Engine] An anti-spam engine is developed using OpenCV Computer Vision libraries, to perform real-time template matching across different screens which accurately detects and prevents spamming.
This provides a safe space for people with IDs to learn socially acceptable ways to communicate and express their thoughts and feelings without offending others.
This project allowed me to further my skills in python, to create a project that benefits the beneficiaries in a positive way.

2020 Science Exploration Challenge
#Leadership
#STEM
The Science Exploration Challenge (SEC) is an annual competition organized by the Science Centre Singapore, aimed at promoting scientific exploration and innovation among primary and secondary school students in Singapore.
The SEC provides students with a unique opportunity to apply their knowledge of science to real-world problems, and to develop skills in scientific inquiry, data analysis, and communication. It also helps to foster a passion for science, and encourages students to pursue careers in STEM fields.
In this competition, we took up a research project with the title “Investigation of the Power Output of Various Batteries”. Using 5 different types of batteries from named brands, we looked at the power output of the batteries over time and determined the quality of everyone of of them. This allowed us to collect over 973 lines of data.
This project taught me the fundamentals of a good experiment and gave me confidence to carry out experiments myself. To find out more, please read the document of look at the spreadsheet.

2020 National robotics Challenge
#Leadership
#Robotics
#STEM
The National Robotics Competition (NRC) organized by Science Centre Singapore is an annual event that showcases the creativity, innovation, and technical skills of young people in Singapore.
We participated in the OPEN category which allowed us to take on real world problems and come up with solutions for them. We undertook the problem statement “how might we generate electricity despite Singapore’s limited land”
Our idea includes have a fleet of small boats with solar panels go out to the sea to generate electricity via solar panels, and return back to the dock at night. At night, they act as floaters thats moves up and down due to stronger wave motions at night, which generates electricity. To showcase these designs, we used a mix of lego and Arduino to bring across the concept.
This competition taught me more about critical thinking. To find out more, please read though the PDF document
